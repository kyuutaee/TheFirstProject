Syllabus

In this course we will provide a thorough and elaborate study of interactive concepts and techniques for applications. We will conduct an extensive investigation into the interface, mechanism, controls, and aims of interactive works. Students will learn how to design and develop complex interactive projects, undertaking comprehensive research and directing their thinking process from brainstorming to final outcome. This course will provide students with the tools to conceive, plan, and develop an interactive system, and they will become aware of the importance of their role in the development of interactive media. Projects

Assignment 1: Dialogue


Choose a conversation between two or more people. They can be interviews, dialogues, debates, etc. Build two pages that feature the same conversation, each designed and styled and typeset differently. With each site, consider your interpretation of the text when setting your type (ie typeface, size, format, margins, etc) and consider how these decisions alter the meaning.

For the first site, make legibility and function you’re main concern. What is the best format for this text that allows the viewer to experience the writing while also helping users understand which person is speaking. Use only one typeface and one color.

For the second site, expression and tone are your main concerns. How would you set the type if you were less concerned about legibility? How can the structure of the text relate to the essay in a more visceral way? It’s still important to know who’s speaking when, but does it have to be legible? Is there a way to convey the original premise and tone the author intended without reading it? Multiple typefaces, shapes, and colors are allowed.


Requirements:

  • Dialogue must be at least 50 words
  • Both sites should be mainly typographic
  • Must include title and author
  • The two sites should be drastically different from one another
